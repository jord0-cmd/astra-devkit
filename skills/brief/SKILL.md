# Brief

Create executive summaries, decision memos, and one-page briefing documents.

## Activation

Use when: user asks for "executive summary", "brief", "briefing", "decision memo",
"one-pager", or needs to distill complex information for leadership.

## Workflow

1. **Source**: What information? (data, research, meeting outcomes, project status)
2. **Audience**: Who reads this? (executive, board, team lead, client)
3. **Draft**: Create structured brief
4. **Deliver**: Save as .docx or .pdf

## Brief Structure

### Executive Brief (Default)
- **Title + Date** (one line)
- **Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF)** — the key message in 1-2 sentences
- **Background** — why this matters (2-3 sentences max)
- **Key Findings / Data** — bullet points with numbers
- **Options / Recommendations** — what you suggest and why
- **Next Steps** — specific actions with owners and dates

### Decision Memo
- **Decision Required** — what needs to be decided
- **Options** — 2-3 options with pros/cons
- **Recommendation** — which option and why
- **Impact** — cost, timeline, risk for each option
- **Deadline** — when the decision is needed

## Rules

- Maximum 1 page for briefs, 2 pages for decision memos
- Lead with the conclusion, not the background
- Use numbers and specifics, not vague statements
- Bold the key takeaway in each section
- Professional, neutral tone — let the data speak

## After Delivery

- "Want me to create a full report behind this brief?"
- "Should I draft a presentation based on these findings?"
- "Want me to write the follow-up email to distribute this?"
